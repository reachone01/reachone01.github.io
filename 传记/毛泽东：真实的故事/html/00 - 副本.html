20 The legend goes that long ago in Wuchang a young man named Xin had a wine shop. One day Xin, a well-known and well-liked fellow, gave wine to a wandering Daoist monk. In gratitude on the wall of the shop the monk drew a picture of a crane that turned out to be magical. Every time when someone clapped their hands the crane would perform graceful dances. The young man was ineffably happy for now his shop was always crowded with people who wanted to gaze upon the miraculous crane while downing a cup or two. But after ten years the Daoist monk again showed up in Wuchang. He dropped in to Xin’s shop, drew forth a flute, played upon it, mounted the crane, and flew off to heaven. To commemorate these happenings Xin’s family built the Yellow Crane Tower upon the site of the shop. According to historical data, the tower was built in 223 CE.
